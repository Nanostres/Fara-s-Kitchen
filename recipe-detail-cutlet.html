Vegetable Cutlet

Crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, these spiced vegetable patties are a popular Indian snack. Made with a mix of vegetables, herbs, and spices, then coated in breadcrumbs and fried to golden perfection, they're perfect for tea time or as an appetizer.

Prep Time

30 minutes

Cook Time

15 minutes

Total Time

45 minutes

Servings

10-12 cutlets

(86 reviews)

Ingredients

For the Cutlet Mixture:

  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans), finely chopped and parboiled
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs (for binding)
  • Salt to taste

For Coating:

  • 2 eggs, beaten (for egg wash) or 2 tbsp all-purpose flour mixed with water (for vegan option)
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs

For Frying:

  • Oil for shallow or deep frying

Instructions

Preparing the Cutlet Mixture

  1. 1

    Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.

  2. 2

    Add minced garlic and grated ginger, and sauté for another minute until fragrant.

  3. 3

    Add the chopped green chilies and parboiled mixed vegetables. Sauté for 2-3 minutes.

  4. 4

    Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 1-2 minutes.

  5. 5

    Turn off the heat and add the mashed potatoes, chopped coriander leaves, and breadcrumbs. Mix everything thoroughly.

  6. 6

    Allow the mixture to cool completely. This makes it easier to shape the cutlets.

Shaping and Coating the Cutlets

  1. 1

    Once the mixture has cooled, divide it into equal portions and shape each portion into a flat, round or oval patty.

  2. 2

    Place the beaten eggs (or flour-water mixture for vegan option) in one bowl and breadcrumbs in another.

  3. 3

    Dip each cutlet first in the egg wash (or flour mixture), ensuring it's fully coated, then coat with breadcrumbs on all sides.

  4. 4

    Press the breadcrumbs gently to ensure they stick to the cutlet. Set aside on a plate.

Frying the Cutlets

  1. 1

    Heat oil in a pan for shallow or deep frying. The oil should be hot but not smoking.

  2. 2

    Carefully place the coated cutlets in the hot oil and fry on medium heat until golden brown and crispy on both sides, about 2-3 minutes per side.

  3. 3

    Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

Serving

Serve hot vegetable cutlets with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup. They make a perfect tea-time snack or appetizer.

Tip: You can prepare the cutlet mixture a day ahead and refrigerate. Just shape, coat, and fry them when you're ready to serve. For a healthier version, you can also bake the cutlets at 375°F (190°C) for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.

Chef's Notes

"The secret to perfect cutlets is to make sure the mixture is well-bound and not too wet. If your mixture feels too moist, add a bit more breadcrumbs to absorb the excess moisture."

"Feel free to customize these cutlets with your favorite vegetables. Beetroot, corn, or even finely chopped mushrooms work beautifully. You can also add some mashed paneer (Indian cottage cheese) to the mixture for added protein and a different texture."

Fara, Kasaragod
