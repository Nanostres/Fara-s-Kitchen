Pakoda

Crispy, savory fritters made with chickpea flour batter and various vegetables, pakodas are a beloved Indian snack perfect for rainy days or evening tea. These golden-brown delights are seasoned with aromatic spices and served with mint or tamarind chutney.

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 servings

(98 reviews)

Ingredients

For the Batter:

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 2 tablespoons rice flour (optional, for extra crispiness)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed (approximately 1/2 to 3/4 cup)

For the Vegetables (choose any):

  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 large potato, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup spinach leaves, chopped
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets, small pieces
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, chopped

For Frying:

  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

Preparing the Batter

  1. 1

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the gram flour, rice flour (if using), turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, carom seeds, baking soda (if using), and salt.

  2. 2

    Gradually add water while whisking to form a smooth, thick batter. The consistency should be thick enough to coat the vegetables but not too runny.

  3. 3

    Let the batter rest for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Preparing the Vegetables

  1. 1

    Wash and prepare your chosen vegetables. Slice onions and potatoes thinly, chop spinach, and break cauliflower into small florets.

  2. 2

    If using potatoes or cauliflower, you can optionally parboil them for 2-3 minutes to ensure they cook through completely when fried.

  3. 3

    Add the chopped green chilies and fresh coriander leaves to the vegetables and mix well.

Making the Pakodas

  1. 1

    Heat oil for deep frying in a kadai or deep pan over medium heat. The oil should be hot but not smoking.

  2. 2

    Add the vegetables to the batter and mix gently to coat them evenly.

  3. 3

    For onion pakodas: Take a small handful of batter-coated onion slices and carefully drop them into the hot oil.

  4. 4

    For mixed vegetable pakodas: Take a spoonful of the batter with vegetables and carefully drop it into the hot oil.

  5. 5

    Fry the pakodas in small batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. This ensures they cook evenly and become crispy.

  6. 6

    Fry until the pakodas turn golden brown and crispy on all sides, about 3-4 minutes, turning occasionally for even cooking.

  7. 7

    Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

  8. 8

    Repeat with the remaining batter and vegetables.

Serving

Serve hot pakodas with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup. They are perfect as a snack with evening tea, especially on rainy days.

Tip: For extra flavor, you can add a pinch of chaat masala or garam masala to the pakodas after frying while they're still hot.

Chef's Notes

"The secret to crispy pakodas is getting the batter consistency right - it should be thick enough to coat the vegetables but not too runny. Adding a little rice flour to the gram flour gives extra crispiness."

"Don't be afraid to experiment with different vegetables and spice combinations. Pakodas are versatile and can be customized to your taste preferences. For a healthier version, you can also try air-frying them."

Fara, Kasaragod
